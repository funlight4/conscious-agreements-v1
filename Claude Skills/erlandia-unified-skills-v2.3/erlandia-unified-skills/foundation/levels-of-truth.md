## The Seven Levels of Truth

### Level 1: Surface Facts (Data Points)
**Nature:** Individual observations, measurements, data
**Example:** "It's raining," "Stock price is $50," "Heart rate is 72"
**Reliability:** High for specific moment
**Scope:** Extremely limited
**Decision Power:** Minimal
**Trap:** Making life decisions on momentary data

### Level 2: Pattern Truths (Trends)
**Nature:** Repeated observations forming patterns
**Example:** "It rains more in winter," "Markets cycle," "Exercise improves health"
**Reliability:** Good within context
**Scope:** Limited timeframe/domain
**Decision Power:** Moderate
**Trap:** Assuming patterns are permanent

### Level 3: Systemic Truths (Relationships)
**Nature:** How elements interact within systems
**Example:** "Ecosystems self-regulate," "Organizations reflect leadership," "Emotions affect health"
**Reliability:** Strong within system
**Scope:** System-wide
**Decision Power:** Significant
**Trap:** Not seeing beyond the system

### Level 4: Principle Truths (Natural Laws)
**Nature:** Consistent principles across domains
**Example:** "Energy follows attention," "Like attracts like," "Evolution through challenge"
**Reliability:** Very high
**Scope:** Cross-domain
**Decision Power:** High
**Trap:** Rigid application without context

### Level 5: Archetypal Truths (Universal Patterns)
**Nature:** Patterns appearing across all cultures/times
**Example:** "Hero's journey," "Death/rebirth cycles," "Masculine/feminine polarity"
**Reliability:** Extremely high
**Scope:** Universal human experience
**Decision Power:** Very high
**Trap:** Missing individual uniqueness

### Level 6: Consciousness Truths (Awareness Itself)
**Nature:** Truths about the nature of consciousness
**Example:** "Awareness aware of itself," "Observer affects observed," "Consciousness evolves"
**Reliability:** Fundamental
**Scope:** All experience
**Decision Power:** Profound
**Trap:** Losing practical grounding

### Level 7: Unity Truth (Ultimate Reality)
**Nature:** The truth that encompasses all
**Example:** "All is One," "Separation is illusion," "Love is fundamental force"
**Reliability:** Absolute
**Scope:** Infinite
**Decision Power:** Ultimate
**Trap:** Bypassing relative truth importance

## Truth Level Navigation Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Decision Context
What level does this decision primarily affect?
- Daily task → Levels 1-2 sufficient
- Life pattern → Levels 3-4 needed
- Purpose/meaning → Levels 5-6 required
- Existence itself → Level 7 relevant

### Step 2: Gather Truths at Multiple Levels
For any decision, collect:
```
L1: What are the facts?
L2: What patterns exist?
L3: What systems are involved?
L4: What principles apply?
L5: What archetypes are active?
L6: What does consciousness reveal?
L7: What serves ultimate unity?
```

### Step 3: Find Highest Relevant Truth
Start from Level 7, work down to find:
- Highest truth that applies
- That you can actually work with
- That serves the decision
- That remains practical

### Step 4: Validate Against Lower Truths
Ensure higher truth decision:
- Doesn't violate necessary lower truths
- Incorporates relevant facts
- Respects patterns
- Works within systems

## Common Truth Level Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Surface Truth Tyranny
**Pattern:** "But it's TRUE that [surface fact]!"
**Problem:** Making big decisions on Level 1 truth
**Example:** "I felt bad once there, so I'll never return"
**Solution:** Check higher level patterns

### Mistake 2: Pattern Worship
**Pattern:** "It's always been this way"
**Problem:** Treating Level 2 as ultimate
**Example:** "Markets always recover" (until they don't)
**Solution:** Understand system changes

### Mistake 3: System Blindness
**Pattern:** "This is how the system works"
**Problem:** Can't see beyond Level 3
**Example:** "You need college for success"
**Solution:** Question system assumptions

### Mistake 4: Principle Rigidity
**Pattern:** "The principle says X, so always X"
**Problem:** Applying Level 4 without context
**Example:** "Always tell truth" (to Nazis asking about hidden refugees?)
**Solution:** Higher truth may override

### Mistake 5: Spiritual Bypassing
**Pattern:** "It's all perfect from Level 7"
**Problem:** Using highest truth to avoid responsibility
**Example:** "It's all One, so suffering doesn't matter"
**Solution:** Honor relative truth importance
