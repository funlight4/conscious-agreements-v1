Templates

A comprehensive 7-phase document system for challenging bank claims and asserting your rights. Each phase builds on the previous, creating a strategic escalation path.

How to Use These Templates

These templates are starting points, not form letters. Every situation is unique.

  • Understand first — Read the Foundation and relevant Domain sections
  • Customize — Adapt each template to your specific situation
  • Document — Keep copies of everything you send
  • Send certified — Create proof of delivery
  • Track deadlines — Note when responses are due

The 7-Phase System

1
Information Gathering & Discovery

Force disclosure of evidence, create paper trail, establish foundation for legal challenge.

Templates: QWR, Debt Validation, FOIA Bundle, Original Documents Request, Chain of Title Request, Privacy Act Request
2
Affidavits & Legal Notices

Establish facts, create legal presumptions, formally notify of violations.

Templates: Affidavit of Truth, Notice of Lack of Consideration, Notice of Criminal Violations, Notice of Disputed Debt
3
Court Filings & Legal Actions

Take offensive legal action, stop foreclosure, clear title.

Templates: Quiet Title Complaint, Motion to Dismiss, Counterclaim, Notice of Removal to Federal Court
4
Criminal & Regulatory Complaints

Apply external pressure through law enforcement and regulators.

Templates: FBI Complaint, DOJ Referral, CFPB Complaint, SEC Complaint
5
Alternative Strategies & Commercial Remedies

Test boundaries of monetary system, alternative approaches.

Templates: Conditional Acceptance, Promissory Note Offset, Walker Todd Affidavit, Estoppel Letter, Indemnification Demand
6
Public Pressure & Exposure

Use public scrutiny and collective action for leverage.

Templates: Press Release, Congressional Petition, Class Action Joinder Notice
7
Settlement & Resolution

Negotiate resolution or controlled exit.

Templates: Settlement Agreement, Deed in Lieu Agreement
Suggested Timeline
Weeks 1-2
Phase 1: Send all information requests, create paper trail
Weeks 3-4
Phase 2: File affidavits, send violation notices, establish disputed status
Weeks 5-8
Phase 3: File quiet title, emergency motions if needed, counterclaims if sued
Weeks 8-12
Phase 4: Criminal complaints, regulatory filings, build external pressure
Ongoing
Phases 5-7: Alternative strategies, public pressure, negotiate resolution

Selection Strategy

Facing Immediate Foreclosure?

Priority: Phase 3 (Emergency TRO) → Phase 2 (Disputed Debt Notice) → Phase 3 (Quiet Title)

Have Time to Prepare?

Priority: All of Phase 1 → All of Phase 2 → Strategic selection from Phase 3-4

Already in Court?

Priority: Phase 3 (Motion to Dismiss) → Phase 3 (Counterclaim) → Emergency TRO if sale scheduled

Maximum Pressure?

Use all Phase 1-2, then select from Phase 3-4 based on their response (or non-response)

Core Principle

The Foundation

This system is built on the principle that promissory notes create money in our debt-based monetary system. The goal is to expose that banks provide no consideration for mortgages — they monetize your promissory note rather than lending pre-existing funds.

Make sure you understand Consideration in Commerce before using these templates.

Critical Warnings
  • These templates are based on theories that have had mixed reception in courts
  • Success varies significantly by jurisdiction and judge
  • Consider legal counsel, especially for court filings
  • Document everything — their failures become your evidence
  • Timing is critical — don't wait until the last minute
  • Some strategies are experimental — understand the risks
