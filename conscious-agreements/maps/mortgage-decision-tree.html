Branch Explanations

Emergency Branch (Left Side)

If foreclosure sale is imminent (within 30 days), you need emergency measures first:

  • TRO/Injunction ‚Äî Ask court to stop the sale while you fight
  • Bankruptcy ‚Äî Last resort creates automatic stay; buys time but has serious consequences

Once you've bought time, join the standard path at Phase 1.

Foundation Understanding (Right Side)

If you have time, start by understanding why your position is valid:

  • How banks actually create money (from your signature)
  • Why your promissory note IS the value
  • The consideration paradox that creates legal vulnerability

This understanding informs everything you do afterward.

Response Analysis

After Phase 1 discovery, their response (or non-response) determines your leverage:

  • No response = RESPA violation, strong leverage
  • Incomplete response = Document gaps, continue pressure
  • Complete response = Rare; analyze for chain of title issues
Court vs. Regulatory Path

At Phase 3-4, you can pursue both paths simultaneously or focus on one:

  • Court path ‚Äî More direct, but requires legal skill or counsel
  • Regulatory path ‚Äî External pressure, creates paper trail, may trigger investigation

Many successful strategies use both in parallel.
