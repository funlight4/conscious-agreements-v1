üìö The Definition Problem

"Definition" means "the meaning or limit/bounds of meaning of a word, term, phrase, etc." ‚Äî Online Etymology Dictionary
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Insight
Anything that does not clearly set boundaries on a meaning is NOT a definition. This is crucial when examining how statutes define "person," "individual," and "citizen."

The "Person" Definition Under Federal Law:

Under 26 USC 7701(a)(1), "Person" includes:

  • An individual
  • A trust, estate, partnership, association
  • Company or corporation
üí£ The Bombshell
"Individual" actually means "Individual proprietorship" which ALSO means "sole proprietorship" - NOT a living human being! The word "individual" in federal tax law refers to a type of business entity.

‚öñÔ∏è Key Case Law

Slaughter House Cases, 83 US 36 (1872)

+

The flowchart calls this "the ONE CASE THAT COULD COMPLETELY CHANGE YOUR LIFE."

  • The 14th Amendment created a NEW type of citizenship - "citizen of the United States" - distinct from state citizenship
  • Before 1871/14th Amendment, "state citizens" would be found - this is "The Republic"
  • There is a citizenship of the United States AND a citizenship of a State, which are distinct
"It is quite clear, then, that there is a citizenship of the United States, and a citizenship of a State, which are distinct from each other, and which depend upon different characteristics or circumstances in the individual."

United States v. Cooper Corp, 312 U.S. 600 (1941)

+

Key Holding: The term "person" does NOT include the sovereign

Since common usage of "person" does not include the sovereign, statutes employing the phrase are ordinarily construed to exclude it.

üí° Application
If you say something is NONPROFIT, the burden of evidence shifts to prosecution to prove it's for profit.

Cruden v. Neale, 2 N.C. (1796)

+
"It is not a rule upon mankind in their natural state. There, every man is independent of all laws, except those prescribed by nature. He is not bound by any institutions formed by his fellowmen without his consent."

This establishes the principle of natural independence from imposed laws without consent.

Hooven & Allison v. Evatt, 324 US 652 (1945)

+

"United States" has MULTIPLE meanings:

  1. The name of a sovereign occupying the position of other nations
  2. The territory over which the sovereignty extends (including territories)
  3. The collective name of the states united by the Constitution

California would be "foreign" from "United States" because that term typically refers to the District of Columbia in federal statutes.

üìú UCC Article 3: Negotiable Instruments

Under UCC 3-104, a negotiable instrument is:

  • A written unconditional promise or order to pay
  • A fixed amount of money
  • Payable on demand or at a definite time
  • Payable to order or to bearer

üìù NOTE

Unconditional PROMISE to pay

  • Promissory notes
  • Federal Reserve Notes
  • Credit card transactions
  • Insurance policies
  • Loan agreements

üìã DRAFT

Unconditional ORDER to pay

  • Traffic tickets
  • Parking tickets
  • Utility bills
  • Checks
  • Court orders
üìå Important
Per UCC 3-104(e), if an instrument falls within the definition of BOTH "note" and "draft," a person entitled to enforce may treat it as either.

üí£ THE NUCLEAR PROVISION: 18 USC 8

The flowchart states:

"All negotiable instruments are 'obligations or other securities of the United States' under 18 USC 8. Which can be proven under 31 USC 1501."
‚ö†Ô∏è If True, This Means:
  • Your mortgage promissory note is a security of the United States
  • Fraudulent handling of your note = federal crime
  • Theft of your note = theft of government securities
  • Dramatically strengthens criminal complaint filings

üí∞ Consideration & Fraud Defenses

UCC 3-303(b) - Consideration
"If you don't feel as though you were given adequate consideration, you have a cause of action!"
UCC 3-305(a)(1)(iii) - Fraud Defense
"Fraud that induced the obligor to sign the instrument with neither knowledge nor reasonable opportunity to learn of its character or its essential terms."
‚úÖ Application to Mortgages
  • You signed believing bank was lending existing money
  • Bank actually created money from your signature
  • You had no knowledge of this
  • You had no reasonable opportunity to learn this
  • The fraud induced you to sign
  • Result: Contract voidable for fraud

‚úçÔ∏è The Signature Strategy

üî• Most Important Section
UCC 3-402(b)(1) provides that you can sign as a representative of a represented person to avoid being the maker or acceptor AND eliminate being liable on the note or draft.

The Separation Technique:

Example from Flowchart:
By: Williams, Brandon Joe, representative
For: BRANDON JOE WILLIAMS, represented person

This creates separation between:

Component Identity Liability
Williams, Brandon Joe Living man (agent/representative) None - acting in representative capacity
BRANDON JOE WILLIAMS Corporate fiction (principal) All liability on the fiction
üìù Important Note
Per UCC 3-402(5), you can always send a NOVATION if you're not happy with the way your signature was accepted.

Special Indorsement States specific payee Only that payee can enforce

This clarifies you are NOT the maker, drawer, or acceptor - you're merely an agent signing on behalf of the represented entity.
