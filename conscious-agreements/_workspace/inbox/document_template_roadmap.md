# Complete Document Template Roadmap
*Every Document You'll Need to Challenge Banking Fraud*

## Overview
This roadmap outlines every document template needed from initial discovery through final resolution. Documents are organized by phase and purpose, with timing and strategic notes.

---

# PHASE 1: INFORMATION GATHERING DOCUMENTS
*Purpose: Establish facts and gather evidence*

## 1.1 Request Documents

### 1.1.1 **Qualified Written Request (QWR)**
- **Required by:** RESPA (12 USC 2605)
- **Send to:** Loan servicer
- **Timeline:** Response required within 30 days
- **Purpose:** Obtain complete loan history and documentation

### 1.1.2 **Debt Validation Letter**
- **Required by:** FDCPA (15 USC 1692g)
- **Send to:** Debt collectors (if applicable)
- **Timeline:** Send within 30 days of first contact
- **Purpose:** Force proof of valid debt

### 1.1.3 **Request for Original Documents**
- **Send to:** Bank/Servicer
- **Purpose:** Demand to see original promissory note with endorsements

### 1.1.4 **Chain of Title Request**
- **Send to:** All parties claiming interest
- **Purpose:** Establish complete ownership history

### 1.1.5 **Securitization Audit Request**
- **Send to:** Bank/Servicer
- **Purpose:** Determine if loan was securitized and obtain PSA

## 1.2 Government Information Requests

### 1.2.1 **FOIA Request - Federal Reserve**
- **Purpose:** Any discount window transactions with your note

### 1.2.2 **FOIA Request - OCC/FDIC**
- **Purpose:** Bank examination reports, complaints

### 1.2.3 **FOIA Request - SEC**
- **Purpose:** Securitization filings if applicable

### 1.2.4 **FOIA Request - CFPB**
- **Purpose:** Prior complaints against bank

### 1.2.5 **Privacy Act Request**
- **Purpose:** All government records about you

## 1.3 Discovery Demands (If in Litigation)

### 1.3.1 **Request for Production of Documents**
### 1.3.2 **Interrogatories**
### 1.3.3 **Request for Admissions**
### 1.3.4 **Deposition Notices**

# PHASE 2: ESTABLISHING YOUR POSITION
*Purpose: Create legal record of facts and position*

## 2.1 Affidavits

### 2.1.1 **Affidavit of Truth - Money Creation**
- **Purpose:** Sworn statement about promissory note as payment
- **Notarization:** Required
- **Record:** At county recorder

### 2.1.2 **Affidavit of Non-Response**
- **Purpose:** Document bank's failure to respond
- **When:** After 30-day period expires

### 2.1.3 **Affidavit of Negative Averment**
- **Purpose:** Challenge bank's standing/ownership
- **Use:** Foreclosure defense

### 2.1.4 **Affidavit of Walker Todd**
- **Purpose:** Expert testimony on money creation (if obtained)

### 2.1.5 **Affidavit of Forensic Audit**
- **Purpose:** Document audit findings
- **Attach:** Full audit report

## 2.2 Notices to Bank

### 2.2.1 **Notice of Lack of Consideration**
- **Send to:** CEO, Board of Directors
- **Purpose:** Demand proof of consideration
- **Timeline:** 30-day cure period

### 2.2.2 **Notice of Disputed Debt**
- **Purpose:** Formally dispute all alleged debt
- **Effect:** Triggers FDCPA protections

### 2.2.3 **Notice of Revocation of Signature**
- **Purpose:** Revoke signature based on fraud
- **Legal basis:** Fraud in the inducement

### 2.2.4 **Notice of Criminal Violations**
- **Purpose:** Put bank on notice of criminal conduct
- **Include:** Specific USC violations

### 2.2.5 **Notice of Intent to File Criminal Complaints**
- **Purpose:** Final warning before criminal filing
- **Timeline:** 10-day final cure period

## 2.3 Public Recordings

### 2.3.1 **Notice of Payment via Promissory Note**
- **Record at:** County recorder
- **Purpose:** Public notice that property is paid

### 2.3.2 **Notice of Fraudulent Claim**
- **Purpose:** Cloud title to prevent transfer

### 2.3.3 **Lis Pendens**
- **Purpose:** Notice of pending litigation
- **When:** Before filing lawsuit

# PHASE 3: DEFAULT AND ESCALATION
*Purpose: Document bank's failure and escalate*

## 3.1 Default Documents

### 3.1.1 **Notice of Default - Failure to Prove Consideration**
- **When:** After 30-day cure expires
- **Effect:** Documents admission of no consideration

### 3.1.2 **Notice of Default - Failure to Validate Debt**
- **When:** No proper validation received
- **Effect:** Debt cannot be collected

### 3.1.3 **Certificate of Non-Response**
- **Purpose:** Formal certification of bank's silence
- **Notarize:** Yes

### 3.1.4 **Notice of Estoppel**
- **Purpose:** Bank is estopped from claiming debt
- **Basis:** Failure to rebut facts

## 3.2 Criminal Complaints

### 3.2.1 **FBI Criminal Complaint**
- **Focus:** White collar crime, banking fraud
- **Statutes:** 18 USC 1025, 1341, 1344

### 3.2.2 **DOJ Criminal Complaint**
- **Focus:** Civil rights, slavery/peonage
- **Statutes:** 18 USC 1581, 1584, 241

### 3.2.3 **US Attorney Criminal Complaint**
- **Focus:** Local federal prosecution
- **Include:** All applicable violations

### 3.2.4 **State Attorney General Complaint**
- **Focus:** State banking law violations
- **Include:** Consumer protection claims

### 3.2.5 **County District Attorney Complaint**
- **Focus:** Local criminal violations
- **Include:** Notary fraud if applicable

## 3.3 Regulatory Complaints

### 3.3.1 **CFPB Complaint**
- **Online:** ConsumerFinance.gov
- **Attach:** All documentation

### 3.3.2 **OCC Complaint** (if national bank)
### 3.3.3 **Federal Reserve Complaint** (if member bank)
### 3.3.4 **FDIC Complaint** (if state bank)
### 3.3.5 **State Banking Commissioner Complaint**
### 3.3.6 **SEC Complaint** (if securities involved)

# PHASE 4: DEMAND DOCUMENTS
*Purpose: Formal demands for remedy*

## 4.1 Settlement Demands

### 4.1.1 **Demand for Discharge of Debt**
- **Basis:** Lack of consideration
- **Timeline:** 10-day response

### 4.1.2 **Demand for Return of All Payments**
- **Basis:** Fraud, unjust enrichment
- **Include:** Calculation of damages

### 4.1.3 **Demand for Clear Title**
- **Purpose:** Remove all liens and clouds

### 4.1.4 **Demand for Reconveyance**
- **Purpose:** Return of deed of trust

## 4.2 Cease and Desist Demands

### 4.2.1 **Cease and Desist Collection Activities**
- **Basis:** Invalid debt
- **Penalty warning:** Criminal violations

### 4.2.2 **Cease and Desist Credit Reporting**
- **Send to:** Bank and credit agencies
- **Basis:** Fraudulent reporting

### 4.2.3 **Cease and Desist Foreclosure**
- **Urgent:** If foreclosure initiated
- **Include:** All violations

# PHASE 5: LITIGATION DOCUMENTS
*Purpose: Court filings for offense or defense*

## 5.1 Defensive Documents (They Sue You)

### 5.1.1 **Answer to Complaint**
- **Timeline:** 20-30 days typically
- **Include:** All affirmative defenses

### 5.1.2 **Affirmative Defenses List**
- Lack of consideration
- Fraud in the inducement
- Unclean hands
- Lack of standing
- Payment already made

### 5.1.3 **Counterclaim**
- **Claims:** Fraud, slavery, RICO, etc.
- **Demand:** Damages and relief

### 5.1.4 **Motion to Dismiss**
- **Basis:** Lack of standing, jurisdiction

### 5.1.5 **Motion for Summary Judgment**
- **Basis:** No genuine issue of material fact

### 5.1.6 **Demand for Jury Trial**
- **Right:** Constitutional guarantee
- **Strategy:** Educate jury on fraud

## 5.2 Offensive Documents (You Sue Them)

### 5.2.1 **Quiet Title Complaint**
- **Purpose:** Clear ownership of property
- **Burden:** On bank to prove interest

### 5.2.2 **Civil RICO Complaint**
- **Basis:** Pattern of mail fraud, extortion
- **Relief:** Triple damages

### 5.2.3 **18 USC 1595 Slavery Civil Action**
- **Basis:** Private right for peonage
- **Relief:** Actual and punitive damages

### 5.2.4 **Declaratory Judgment Action**
- **Purpose:** Court declares rights
- **Seek:** Declaration of no debt

### 5.2.5 **Injunction Request/TRO**
- **Emergency:** Stop foreclosure
- **Basis:** Irreparable harm

# CRITICAL REMINDERS

## Document Requirements
✓ **Everything in writing** - No verbal agreements  
✓ **Certified mail** - Return receipt requested  
✓ **Notarization** - For all affidavits  
✓ **Multiple copies** - Original + 3 minimum  
✓ **Digital backups** - Cloud and physical  

## Strategic Considerations
- **Timing matters** - Follow statutory deadlines
- **Build record** - Each document builds case
- **Stay factual** - No threats or speculation
- **Professional tone** - Credibility matters
- **Legal review** - Consult attorney when possible
