## LEGAL BASIS FOR DEMAND

**UCC ¬ß 3-305(c) - Claims to the Instrument**  
I am entitled to assert claims of other parties to the instrument as a defense.

**UCC ¬ß 3-602 - Payment**  
Payment to a party not entitled to enforce the instrument does not discharge the obligation.

**Common Law - Risk of Double Liability**  
I have the right to ensure I am paying the correct party and to be protected from multiple claims.

**Due Process**  
I have the constitutional right to know who my actual creditor is before being forced to pay.

**Real Party in Interest**  
Only the real party in interest has standing to collect or foreclose.

## üîç WHY THIS WORKS

### The Securitization Shell Game:

When mortgages are securitized:
1. Note sold to depositor
2. Depositor sells to trust
3. Trust issues certificates to investors
4. Servicer collects payments
5. MERS claims to track ownership

**Problem:** Each transfer may be defective, creating multiple potential claimants.

### Your Rights:
- Pay only the true owner
- Know who owns your debt
- Protection from double payment
- Clear chain of title
- Proper legal documentation

### Their Problem:
- Can't prove ownership
- Multiple parties involved
- Lost original notes
- Defective transfers
- Conflicting claims

## ‚öñÔ∏è LEGAL PRECEDENTS

### Supporting Cases:
- **Carpenter v. Longan** - Note and mortgage must stay together
- **Ibanez** (Mass.) - Must prove ownership to foreclose
- **Glaski** (Cal.) - Late transfers void
- **Schwartzwald** (Ohio) - Standing required at filing

### Key Principle:
"The party seeking to foreclose must be the owner and holder of the note and mortgage at the time the action is filed."
