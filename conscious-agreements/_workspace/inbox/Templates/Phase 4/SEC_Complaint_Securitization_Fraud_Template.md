## SECURITIZATION FRAUD SCHEME

### The fraudulent scheme involves:

### 1. False Representations to Investors:
- Misrepresenting loan quality and underwriting standards
- Concealing that loans lacked proper documentation
- Hiding that no actual money was lent (lack of consideration)
- Falsely claiming loans met REMIC requirements

### 2. Chain of Title Fraud:
- Notes never properly transferred to trusts
- Assignments backdated to meet REMIC deadlines
- Robo-signed endorsements and assignments
- Multiple sales of same loans to different trusts

### 3. REMIC Violations:
- Loans not transferred within 90-day window
- Defective loans not properly substituted
- Trust engaged in prohibited transactions
- False tax certifications to IRS

### 4. Ongoing Fraud in Servicing:
- Foreclosing without proper ownership
- Manufacturing documents to cover fraud
- Misrepresenting to investors about loan performance
- Improper use of insurance and settlement proceeds

## SPECIFIC MISREPRESENTATIONS TO INVESTORS

### 1. Prospectus and Offering Documents

The offering documents contained material misrepresentations including:
- Claimed loans met underwriting guidelines when they did not
- Stated loan-to-value ratios were accurate when inflated appraisals were used
- Represented that all loans had proper documentation
- Claimed true sales when loans never properly transferred
- Misrepresented that consideration was provided for loans
- Failed to disclose conflicts of interest
- Concealed that trusts could not foreclose due to broken chains of title

### 2. Loan Quality Misrepresentations

The defendants misrepresented loan quality by:
- Claiming rigorous underwriting when none existed
- Hiding that many loans were already in default
- Concealing predatory lending practices
- Misrepresenting borrower creditworthiness
- Failing to disclose that loans were fraudulently originated
- Hiding lack of consideration (no money actually lent)

### 3. Due Diligence Fraud

The defendants corrupted the due diligence process by:
- Ignoring negative due diligence findings
- Cherry-picking loans for review
- Pressuring due diligence firms to overlook defects
- Waiving loans flagged as defective into pools
- Destroying or concealing adverse reports
- Preventing investors from conducting independent review
