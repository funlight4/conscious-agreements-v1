**Power Move:** Transform from defendant to counter-plaintiff, seeking monetary damages and injunctive relief.

## üí° STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Claim Selection Strategy:

**Always Include:**
- FDCPA (if debt collector)
- FCRA (if credit reporting issues)
- RESPA (if QWR sent)
- Fraud (foundation for all)

**Consider Including:**
- RICO (if pattern of violations)
- State consumer protection laws
- Emotional distress
- Punitive damages claims

### Discovery Opportunities:

Once counterclaim filed, you can demand:
- Internal bank communications
- Securitization documents
- Accounting records
- Robo-signing evidence
- Pattern of similar conduct

### Settlement Leverage:

Counterclaims create pressure because:
- Bank must defend (expensive)
- Risk of jury verdict
- Potential class action
- Bad publicity risk
- Regulatory scrutiny

## üéØ WHY COUNTERCLAIMS MATTER

### Offensive Position:
- You're no longer just defending
- You're seeking damages
- They must respond/defend
- Costs them money/time

### Discovery Rights:
- Broader discovery scope
- Can depose their employees
- Access internal documents
- Uncover systemic fraud

### Settlement Dynamics:
- Creates mutual risk
- Encourages negotiation
- Offset potential
- Walk-away deals possible

### Jury Appeal:
- Juries sympathize with homeowners
- Banks are unsympathetic defendants
- David vs. Goliath narrative
- Punitive damages possible
