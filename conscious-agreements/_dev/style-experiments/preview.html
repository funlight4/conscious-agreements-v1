Words That Don't Mean What You Think

PERSON
Black's Law Dictionary, 4th Edition
In law, a "person" includes corporations, associations, firms, partnerships, societies, and other entities ‚Äî NOT just human beings. When a statute says "person," it often means a legal fiction, not you the living man or woman.
The Aha Moment

When you signed that mortgage, you created a promissory note ‚Äî an IOU worth $300,000. The bank didn't give you their money. They took YOUR note, deposited it as an asset, and gave you back... credit based on your own promise. You paid. They just forgot to tell you.

This Is Not About Escaping

Understanding agreements isn't about declaring yourself "sovereign" or claiming laws don't apply. It's about conscious participation ‚Äî knowing what game you're in, what rules you're playing by, and what choices you actually have. Education, not confrontation.

Wait, really?

Every U.S. dollar says "Federal Reserve NOTE" on it. That's because it IS a note ‚Äî a promise to pay. Since 1971 (when gold backing ended), these notes represent promises to pay... other notes. It's promises all the way down.

How We Talk About This

We're not here to scare you or make you angry. This isn't about "evil banks" or conspiracy theories. It's about understanding a system that operates on specific definitions and precise procedures that most people never learn.

The Simple Version

Imagine signing up for a gym membership while sleepwalking. You wake up, you're being charged monthly, and when you complain, they show you your signature. "But I didn't know what I was signing!" you say. "Ignorance is no excuse," they reply.

That's basically how the legal system works. Except instead of a gym, it's citizenship, contracts, and debt. And instead of sleepwalking, it's just... never being taught the rules.
