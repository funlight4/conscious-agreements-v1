The Name

Is there a difference between "JOHN SMITH" and "John Smith"? The ALL CAPITALS name theory suggests these may refer to different legal entities — one a living being, the other a legal fiction.

The Observation

Look at official documents. Notice how your name appears:

JOHN DAVID SMITH

Compare this to how you write your own name:

John David Smith

The ALL CAPITALS format appears consistently on:

  • Driver's licenses
  • Birth certificates
  • Social Security cards
  • Court documents
  • Tax forms
  • Bank statements
  • Credit cards
  • Bills and invoices

Is this merely a formatting convention, or does it signify something legally significant?

The Theory

The "strawman" or "artificial person" theory proposes:

Format What It Represents
JOHN SMITH
(All capitals)
  • Ens legis — "a being of the law," a legal entity
  • The corporate fiction / "person" / artificial entity
  • Created at birth registration
  • Identified by Social Security Number
  • Subject to statutory and administrative law
John Smith
(Upper/lowercase)
  • The living man or woman
  • Flesh and blood, soul and spirit
  • Exists independent of government registration
  • Possesses inherent, unalienable rights
  • Subject to natural law and common law
Ens Legis
Black's Law Dictionary

"A creature of the law; an artificial being, as contrasted with a natural person. Applied to corporations, considered as deriving their existence entirely from the law."

How the Entity Is Created

According to this theory:

  1. Birth occurs — A living child is born
  2. Birth is registered — Parents file a birth certificate
  3. State creates a record — An entry is made in vital records
  4. Certificate is issued — A document with the name in ALL CAPITALS
  5. SSN is assigned — The entity receives a tax identification number
  6. Entity is activated — The legal fiction can now transact

The living child and the registered entity are not the same thing — they merely share a name (in different formats).

The Bonding Theory

Some extend this theory to suggest that birth certificates are securities — bonds traded on exchanges, with the newborn's future labor as collateral. This is one of the more contested claims and has been repeatedly rejected by courts. Proceed with caution.

The Connection to Commerce

This connects to signature liability:

  • When you sign as "John Smith," you may be acting as the living being
  • When you sign for "JOHN SMITH," you may be acting as representative of the entity
  • How you sign determines which capacity — and which liabilities — apply

Under UCC 3-402, signing as a representative for an identified represented person can shield you from personal liability. If "JOHN SMITH" is the represented person and you (the living man) are merely the representative...

Example Signature Format
By: Smith, John, representative
For: JOHN SMITH, represented person
Without recourse
All rights reserved, UCC 1-308

Evidence and Arguments

Supporting Observations

  • The consistent use of ALL CAPS on government documents is unusual if merely stylistic
  • Legal style guides typically use proper capitalization for names
  • The UCC provides for representative signatures, suggesting a separation is possible
  • Courts have acknowledged that "person" doesn't always mean "human being"

Counter-Arguments

  • No statute explicitly defines ALL CAPS as meaning a different legal entity
  • Courts routinely dismiss "strawman" arguments as frivolous
  • Government agencies claim it's merely a data formatting convention
  • Many who have tried these arguments have faced adverse consequences

Practical Considerations

Observational Awareness

  • Notice how your name appears on different documents
  • Pay attention to which "you" is being addressed
  • Consider what capacity you're acting in when you sign

When Signing Documents

  • Consider whether representative signature format is appropriate
  • Know that institutions may reject non-standard signatures
  • Weigh the practical consequences against theoretical protection

In Legal Proceedings

  • Be cautious about making "strawman" arguments in court
  • Understand that judges are often hostile to these theories
  • Focus on procedural and substantive arguments that courts accept

Strong Warnings

Court Hostility

Courts have consistently rejected "strawman" and "artificial person" arguments. Examples of consequences:

  • Cases dismissed with prejudice
  • Sanctions for frivolous filings
  • Contempt findings
  • Criminal charges in extreme cases
  • Loss of credibility for other valid arguments

The value of understanding this theory is primarily:

  • Awareness — Seeing how the system may operate
  • Questioning — Asking who is being addressed
  • Capacity awareness — Understanding representative vs. personal signing
  • Framework — A lens for viewing legal interactions

It's NOT a reliable strategy for:

  • Avoiding taxes
  • Escaping valid debts
  • Evading court jurisdiction
  • Nullifying contracts you've willingly entered
