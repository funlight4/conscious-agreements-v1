The Three Keys

Everything is Agreement

What looks like "law" is usually contract. What feels like obligation is often consent you didn't know you gave. Agreements require two parties ‚Äî and you're always one of them.

Containers Have Boundaries

Every "game" operates inside a container. Step inside, the rules apply. Stay outside, they don't. The trick is knowing which container you're in ‚Äî and how you got there.

Consideration is Everything

For a contract to be valid, both sides must exchange something of value. Without consideration, there's no deal. This one principle invalidates most unconscious "agreements."

Wait, really?

Every U.S. dollar says "Federal Reserve NOTE" on it. That's because it IS a note ‚Äî a promise to pay. Your signature on a promissory note creates the same thing. When you "borrow" money, you're actually creating it. The bank just doesn't mention that part.

The Journey Ahead

This isn't about "beating the system" or magic words that make debts disappear. It's about understanding ‚Äî seeing the agreements you're already in, learning how you got there, and discovering what choices you actually have. The goal isn't escape. It's conscious participation.

This is education, not legal advice. Understanding agreements doesn't mean escaping all obligations ‚Äî it means knowing what you've agreed to and having clarity about your options. When in doubt, do more research. When really in doubt, find someone who's walked the path before you.
