Mortgage Challenges

Understanding mortgage agreements through the consideration lens, and a systematic approach for documenting and challenging bank claims when you believe the contract lacks validity.

Critical Warning

Challenging a mortgage is serious. Improper actions can result in:

  • Foreclosure and loss of your home
  • Damaged credit
  • Court sanctions for frivolous filings
  • Potential criminal liability if done improperly

Consider professional legal counsel before taking action.

The Core Issue

As explored in Money Creation and Consideration in Commerce, the fundamental question is:

"What consideration did the bank provide for your promissory note?"

If the bank created the "loan" from your promissory note — rather than lending pre-existing funds — the consideration question becomes relevant to contract validity.

However, courts have largely rejected this argument. The strategic approach focuses on:

  1. Documentation — Create a record of your position
  2. Standing challenges — Can they prove they can enforce?
  3. Procedural violations — Did they follow required laws?
  4. Negotiation leverage — Knowledge creates options

The Administrative Process

1
Information Gathering
Timeline: 30-60 days

Collect Your Documents

  • Original loan application
  • Promissory note (copy)
  • Deed of trust / mortgage
  • Closing documents (HUD-1 or Closing Disclosure)
  • All correspondence from lender/servicer
  • Complete payment history
  • Current statements

Send Qualified Written Request (QWR)

Under RESPA (12 USC 2605), servicers must respond within 30 days. Request:

  • Complete payment history from origination
  • All documents related to loan origination
  • Proof of current ownership of the promissory note
  • Complete chain of title for the note
  • All assignments of the mortgage/deed of trust
  • Documentation of any securitization
  • Pooling and servicing agreement if securitized

Send via certified mail, return receipt requested.

Send FOIA/Privacy Act Requests

To relevant government agencies:

  • Federal Reserve (discount window activity)
  • OCC (if national bank)
  • FDIC (if FDIC insured)
  • CFPB (any complaints/investigations)
  • SEC (if securitized)
2
Establishing the Record
Timeline: 30 days after Phase 1

Create Affidavit of Truth

A sworn statement of your position, notarized. Include:

  • Facts of the transaction
  • Your understanding of promissory notes as payment
  • Your position on consideration
  • Reservation of all rights

Notice of Lack of Consideration

Send to bank CEO and board requesting proof of:

  • Lawful consideration from their institution
  • Documentation of pre-existing funds lent
  • Evidence they suffered detriment or loss

Give 30-day cure period. Send certified to CEO and board members.

Public Recording

Consider recording at county recorder:

  • Affidavit of Truth
  • Notice of Payment via Promissory Note

Medium Risk — Some recorders may reject; others accept. Creates public record of your position.

3
Administrative Escalation
Timeline: After 30-day cure period

If No Adequate Response

Send Notice of Default documenting their failure to:

  • Prove lawful consideration
  • Produce requested documents
  • Respond within required timeframes

Regulatory Complaints

File simultaneously with:

  • CFPB (consumerfinance.gov) — Attach all documents
  • State Banking Commissioner — Request investigation
  • State Attorney General — Consumer protection division

Consider Criminal Complaints

Higher Risk — May be seen as frivolous if not well-documented

  • FBI White Collar Crime Division
  • DOJ Criminal Division, Fraud Section
  • US Attorney for your district

Only if you have strong evidence of fraud, not merely disagreement about consideration.

4
Protective Measures
Ongoing throughout process

Document Everything

  • All letters sent (keep certified mail receipts)
  • All responses received
  • Phone logs (date, time, person, content)
  • Record calls if legal in your state
  • Screenshots of online accounts
  • Backup everything in 3 places

If Facing Foreclosure

  • Lis Pendens — Notice of pending litigation
  • Injunction Request — Ask court to stop foreclosure
  • Bankruptcy — Creates automatic stay (emergency measure)
  • Legal Counsel — Get professional help immediately

Key Arguments

Stronger Arguments

  • Standing — Can they prove they hold the note and can enforce it?
  • Chain of Title — Are all assignments proper and complete?
  • RESPA Violations — Did they respond to your QWR properly?
  • TILA Violations — Were all disclosures proper at origination?
  • Robo-signing — Were documents fraudulently executed?

Weaker Arguments (in most courts)

  • Lack of consideration — Courts generally accept that credit extension is consideration
  • Promissory note as payment — Courts typically reject this
  • Money creation theory — Often dismissed as "sovereign citizen" argument

The stronger arguments create the practical leverage; the weaker arguments provide understanding of what actually happened.

Success Indicators

Signs of Progress
  • Bank can't answer consideration demand
  • They offer modification
  • They go silent on collection
  • Regulatory agency opens investigation
  • Principal reduction offered
  • Quiet dismissal of foreclosure
Victory Conditions
  • Promissory note returned marked "PAID"
  • Clear title issued
  • Favorable settlement agreement
  • Restitution of payments
  • Loan modification with significant reduction
