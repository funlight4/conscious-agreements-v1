SECURITIZATION FRAUD SCHEME

The fraudulent scheme involves:

  1. False Representations to Investors:
    • Misrepresenting loan quality and underwriting standards
    • Concealing that loans lacked proper documentation
    • Hiding that no actual money was lent (lack of consideration)
    • Falsely claiming loans met REMIC requirements
  2. Chain of Title Fraud:
    • Notes never properly transferred to trusts
    • Assignments backdated to meet REMIC deadlines
    • Robo-signed endorsements and assignments
    • Multiple sales of same loans to different trusts
  3. REMIC Violations:
    • Loans not transferred within 90-day window
    • Defective loans not properly substituted
    • Trust engaged in prohibited transactions
    • False tax certifications to IRS
  4. Ongoing Fraud in Servicing:
    • Foreclosing without proper ownership
    • Manufacturing documents to cover fraud
    • Misrepresenting to investors about loan performance
    • Improper use of insurance and settlement proceeds
