VI. SPECIFIC FRAUDULENT SCHEME

A. The Money Creation Fraud

Defendants systematically defrauded borrowers by:

  1. Misrepresenting loan transactions: Claimed to lend pre-existing money when actually creating bookkeeping entries from borrowers' promissory notes
  2. Concealing true nature: Failed to disclose that borrower's signature created the funds
  3. Double-dipping: Charged interest on money created from borrower's own promise
  4. Securities fraud: Sold same loans multiple times through securitization

B. The Foreclosure Fraud

When borrowers defaulted on these fraudulent loans, defendants:

  1. Fabricated standing: Created false assignments backdated
  2. Robo-signed documents: Mass-produced fraudulent affidavits
  3. Lost note fraud: Claimed notes lost when actually destroyed/sold
  4. Dual tracking: Foreclosed while negotiating modifications
  5. Fee pyramiding: Added fraudulent fees to increase debt
