This document forces the servicer/bank to provide a binding statement about your loan that they CANNOT change later. Once they certify specific facts, they are legally "estopped" (prevented) from claiming otherwise. This:

  • Locks in their position - Can't change story later
  • Reveals discrepancies - Errors and fraud exposed
  • Creates evidence - Binding admissions for court
  • Prevents surprises - No hidden fees or claims
  • Establishes facts - For negotiation or litigation

LEGAL EFFECT OF ESTOPPEL CERTIFICATE

By providing this certificate, you are making binding representations that:

  1. All information provided is true, accurate, and complete
  2. You are legally estopped from later claiming different amounts or facts
  3. Third parties may rely on this certificate
  4. You may be liable for damages caused by false information
  5. This creates an enforceable agreement as to the stated facts

Legal Authority:

  • Common law doctrine of estoppel
  • UCC Â§ 3-602 - Payment and discharge
  • Contractual duty of good faith
  • State consumer protection laws

REQUIRED CERTIFICATE FORMAT

The Estoppel Certificate must be:

  1. Sworn and Notarized - Under penalty of perjury
  2. On Company Letterhead - Official document
  3. Signed by Authorized Officer - With printed name and title
  4. Dated - As of specific date requested
  5. Complete - All requested information included
  6. With Supporting Documents - Payment history, assignments, etc.

ðŸ“‹ INSTRUCTIONS FOR USE

When to Use:

  • Before making final payment
  • During settlement negotiations
  • Before refinancing or selling
  • When disputing amounts owed
  • To lock in their position
  • Before legal proceedings

How to Send:

  1. Fill in all blanks completely
  2. Calculate 15 business day deadline
  3. Have notarized
  4. Send Certified Mail, Return Receipt
  5. Send to: Legal department, CEO, and regular servicing
  6. Keep all receipts and copies

What to Expect:

  • Full response - Rare but powerful
  • Partial response - Note what's missing
  • Refuse - Document refusal
  • Ignore - Silence = agreement
  • False information - Evidence of fraud

Follow-Up Actions:

  1. After 15 days, send Notice of Default/Agreement
  2. Use their silence as agreement to your facts
  3. Compare response to other documents
  4. Look for discrepancies and fraud
  5. Use in court proceedings
