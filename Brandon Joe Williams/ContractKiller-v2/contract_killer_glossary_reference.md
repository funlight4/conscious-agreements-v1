## Legal Terms Glossary

### A

**Acceptance**: Agreement to terms of an offer, creating a binding contract. In UCC-3, accepting a negotiable instrument creates obligation to pay.

**Acceptance for Value**: Process of accepting a presumed debt/obligation and returning it as payment, based on UCC principles.

**Adhesion Contract**: A "take it or leave it" contract with no negotiation, often unconscionable.

**Affiant**: Person who makes an affidavit; one who swears to the truth of statements.

**Affidavit**: Written statement of facts sworn to be true under penalty of perjury.

**Affirmative Defense**: Defense that admits conduct but avoids liability based on legal excuse/justification.

**Allodial Title**: Absolute property ownership free from any superior landlord.

**Ambassador**: Diplomatic representative; Brandon uses this for members of his Amnesty Coalition.

**Amnesty**: Pardon granted to groups; Brandon's organization name implies forgiveness/fresh start.

### B

**Beneficial Interest**: Right to benefits from a trust or contract while legal title is held by another.

**Benefit**: In contracts, what the promisor receives in exchange for their promise.

**Black's Law Dictionary**: Authoritative legal dictionary; Brandon emphasizes using 4th edition or older.

**Bouvier's Law Dictionary**: Historical legal dictionary; Brandon considers it more accurate than modern sources.

### C

**Capacity**: Legal ability to enter contracts; minors and incompetents lack capacity.

**Capitalization**: ALL CAPS represents corporate/legal entity; Proper Case represents living being.

**Certificate of Live Birth**: Document creating legal person; not the same as birth certificate.

**Citizen**: Member of a political community; Brandon distinguishes state citizen from US citizen.

**Civil Rights**: Privileges granted by government; different from natural/inherent rights.

**Color of Law**: Appearance of legal authority without actual authority.

**Commercial Activity**: Business/trade for profit; driving commercially requires license.

**Common Law**: Law derived from judicial decisions rather than statutes; law of the land.

**Competent**: Having legal capacity; able to understand nature and consequences of actions.

**Conditional Acceptance**: Accepting an offer with modifications; creates counteroffer.

**Consideration**: Something of value exchanged; essential element of valid contract.

**Contract**: Agreement between parties creating obligations enforceable by law.

**Corporation**: Legal entity separate from its owners; Brandon says government operates as corporation.

### D

**De Facto**: In fact/reality, regardless of legal status.

**De Jure**: By law/right; legitimate according to law.

**Discharge**: Satisfying an obligation; in UCC-3, can be done without Federal Reserve Notes.

**District of Columbia**: Federal enclave; Brandon says federal jurisdiction limited to this area.

**Domicile**: Permanent home; legal residence for jurisdictional purposes.

**Driver**: One operating vehicle commercially; Brandon distinguishes from traveler.

**Due Process**: Constitutional requirement of fair legal procedures.

**Duress**: Unlawful pressure to enter agreement; voids contract.

### E

**EIN (Employer Identification Number)**: Federal tax ID; Brandon uses for Title 8 organizations.

**Ens Legis**: Legal person/entity created by law; the statutory person.

**Enfranchisement**: Grant of rights/privileges; Brandon says licenses are enfranchisement.

**Estoppel**: Prevented from denying/asserting something due to previous actions.

**Ex Parte**: One-sided; proceeding without notice to other party.

### F

**Federal Reserve Note (FRN)**: US currency; Brandon calls them debt instruments, not money.

**Fiduciary**: One who acts for another's benefit in position of trust.

**Foreign**: Outside of; Brandon uses "foreign to the United States" strategically.

**Fraud**: Intentional deception causing damage; can be by omission or commission.

**Frivolous**: In law, meant to harass without merit; serious accusation Brandon says to challenge.

### G

**General Appearance**: Submitting to court's jurisdiction unconditionally.

**Good Faith**: Honest dealing; required in contracts under UCC.

### H

**Holder**: Person in possession of negotiable instrument.

**Holder in Due Course**: Holder who takes instrument for value, in good faith, without notice of defects.

### I

**In Personam**: Against the person; personal jurisdiction.

**In Rem**: Against property; jurisdiction over thing rather than person.

**Income**: Brandon says poorly defined in tax law; not wages but profit/gain.

**Indorsement**: Signature on negotiable instrument; different types create different obligations.

**Individual**: Single human being; Brandon distinguishes from "person."

**Involuntary Servitude**: Forced labor; 13th Amendment prohibition.

### J

**Jurisdiction**: Authority to hear and decide cases; Brandon challenges federal jurisdiction.

**Juristic Person**: Artificial person recognized by law; corporations, trusts, etc.

### L

**Legal**: According to law/statute; Brandon distinguishes from "lawful."

**Lawful**: According to natural/common law; Brandon says superior to "legal."

**Lien**: Legal claim against property for debt payment.

**Living Soul**: Brandon's term for natural person vs. legal fiction.

### M

**Man/Woman**: Natural person; Brandon uses instead of "individual" or "person."

**Maritime Law**: Law of the sea; Brandon claims improperly applied on land.

**Meeting of the Minds**: Mutual understanding required for valid contract.

### N

**Natural Person**: Human being; as opposed to artificial/juristic person.

**Negotiable Instrument**: Written promise/order to pay that can be transferred.

**Non-Resident Alien**: Tax status; not resident or citizen of United States.

**Novation**: Substitution of new contract for existing one.

**Null and Void**: Having no legal force or effect.

### O

**Obligation**: Duty to perform; what binds parties in contract.

**Offer**: Proposal to enter contract; must be definite and communicated.

**Organization**: Brandon uses Title 8 definition for unincorporated associations.

### P

**Person**: Legal entity including individuals and organizations; not limited to humans.

**Peonage**: Debt slavery; forcing work to pay debt; illegal under federal law.

**Plaintiff**: Party bringing lawsuit; one claiming injury.

**PMA (Private Membership Association)**: Organization operating under right to associate.

**Power of Attorney**: Authority to act for another; Brandon creates between himself and legal entity.

**Prejudice**: Damage/detriment; "without prejudice" preserves rights.

**Presumption**: Assumption accepted as true until rebutted.

**Private**: Not public; Brandon emphasizes private vs. public capacity.

**Privilege**: Special advantage granted; not a right.

**Pro Se**: Representing oneself in court; without attorney.

**Promissory Note**: Written promise to pay specific amount.

### Q

**Quasi**: As if; resembling but not actually being.

**Quo Warranto**: By what authority; challenge to official's right to hold office.

### R

**Ratification**: Formal approval; Brandon claims 14th Amendment wasn't properly ratified.

**Recourse**: Right to demand payment; "without recourse" limits liability.

**Resident**: One who resides in place; creates tax obligations.

**Revocation**: Withdrawal of offer/power; canceling previous grant.

**Right**: Something inherent that cannot be taken away; vs. privilege.

### S

**Secured Party**: One with security interest in property; creditor with collateral.

**Security Agreement**: Contract creating security interest.

**Signature**: Any mark made with intent to authenticate; creates obligations.

**Sovereign**: Supreme authority; Brandon avoids term "sovereign citizen" as contradictory.

**Special Appearance**: Appearing in court only to challenge jurisdiction.

**State Citizen**: Citizen of one of the 50 states; Brandon distinguishes from US citizen.

**Statute**: Law enacted by legislature; Brandon says only applies to certain persons.

**Strawman**: Legal fiction/artificial person created by government; ALL CAPS name.

**Sui Juris**: Of one's own right; having full legal capacity.

### T

**Taxpayer**: Person subject to tax; Brandon says specific legal definition excludes many.

**Title 8**: Immigration and Nationality law; Brandon uses for organization definition.

**Title 26**: Internal Revenue Code; tax law.

**Tort**: Civil wrong not arising from contract; personal injury.

**Travel**: Right of movement; Brandon distinguishes from commercial driving.

**Trust**: Legal arrangement where trustee holds property for beneficiary.

**Trustee**: Person who holds and manages trust property.

### U

**UCC (Uniform Commercial Code)**: State laws governing commercial transactions.

**UCC-1**: Financing statement; creates public notice of security interest.

**UCC-3**: Article governing negotiable instruments.

**Ultra Vires**: Beyond the powers; act exceeding authority.

**Unconscionable**: So unfair that conscience is shocked; may void contract.

**Unincorporated Association**: Group without corporate status; Title 8 organization.

**United States**: Brandon distinguishes federal corporation from united States of America.

**US Citizen**: Federal citizenship created by 14th Amendment per Brandon.

### V

**Value**: Consideration sufficient to support contract.

**Venue**: Geographic location where case is heard.

**Void**: Having no legal effect from beginning.

**Voidable**: Valid until party chooses to void it.

**Voluntary**: Acting of free will without coercion.

### W

**Waiver**: Voluntary relinquishment of known right.

**Without Prejudice**: Preserving all rights; not final.

**Without Recourse**: Limiting liability on negotiable instrument.

### Z

**ZIP Code**: Brandon claims creates federal jurisdiction presumption.

## Key Legal Codes Quick Reference

### Title 8 - Immigration and Nationality

**8 USC 1101(a)(21)**: Definition of "national"
- Owes permanent allegiance to a state

**8 USC 1101(a)(22)**: Definition of "national of the United States"
- Citizen or non-citizen national

**8 USC 1101(a)(28)**: Definition of "organization"
- Any group, incorporated or not

**8 USC 1101(a)(14)**: Definition of "foreign state"
- Any state not the United States

**8 USC 1401**: Who are citizens
- Lists ways citizenship is acquired

**8 USC 1481**: Loss of nationality
- How citizenship can be lost

### Title 26 - Internal Revenue Code

**26 USC 7701(a)(1)**: Definition of "person"
- Individual, trust, estate, partnership, corporation

**26 USC 7701(a)(14)**: Definition of "taxpayer"
- Any person subject to any internal revenue tax

**26 USC 7701(a)(9)**: Definition of "United States"
- States and District of Columbia

**26 USC 7701(a)(10)**: Definition of "State"
- Includes DC where not otherwise distinct

**26 USC 7701(b)**: Definition of resident/nonresident alien
- Complex rules for tax status

**26 CFR 1.871-1**: Non-resident alien taxation
- Only US source income taxable

### Title 18 - Crimes

**18 USC 911**: False claim to US citizenship
- Crime to falsely claim citizenship

**18 USC 1581**: Peonage
- Holding person in debt servitude

**18 USC 241**: Conspiracy against rights
- Two or more conspiring to violate rights

**18 USC 242**: Deprivation of rights under color of law
- Using official position to violate rights

### Title 42 - Public Health and Welfare

**42 USC 1983**: Civil action for deprivation of rights
- Lawsuit for constitutional violations

**42 USC 1985**: Conspiracy to interfere with civil rights
- Civil remedy for conspiracy

**42 USC 1986**: Neglect to prevent conspiracy
- Liability for failing to stop violation

### UCC - Uniform Commercial Code

**UCC 1-308**: Reservation of rights
- Former 1-207; preserving rights

**UCC 3-104**: Negotiable instruments
- Requirements for negotiability

**UCC 3-302**: Holder in due course
- Requirements and rights

**UCC 3-305**: Defenses to payment
- What defenses can be raised

**UCC 3-401**: Signature
- Who is liable on instrument

**UCC 3-501**: Presentment
- Demand for payment or acceptance

**UCC 3-601**: Discharge
- How obligation is discharged

## Quick Decision Trees

### "Are You a US Citizen?"
1. If maintaining system: "Yes"
2. If transitioning: "I'm a state citizen"
3. If fully transitioned: "I'm foreign to the United States"

### Received Government Notice
1. Is there a valid contract? → Check consideration
2. Is jurisdiction proper? → Challenge if not
3. Is notice proper? → Require clarification
4. Response required? → Conditional acceptance

### Traffic Stop
1. Am I traveling or driving? → Traveling (non-commercial)
2. Do I consent? → No
3. Am I free to go? → Ask repeatedly
4. If detained → Document everything

### Tax Document Request
1. W-9 requested → Provide W-8BEN instead
2. Tax return required? → File 1040NR or challenge
3. Levy threatened? → Challenge taxpayer status
